The Hotel BFF Journey (5 Stages)

Hotel BFF frames their product as a journey with five stages. The acronym spells out a progression: Ascertain, Train, Obtain, Attain, Maintain. This is their core product narrative.

Key Insights from CB Call (April 14, 2026)

CB called to discuss how the engagement should work. Several important strategic points emerged.

Product Evolution

Hotel BFF has expanded beyond GSTC certification into 6-7 standalone modules (energy, carbon, waste, water, biodiversity, profitability). Hotels can buy individual modules or the full certification package. Prototypes exist for all modules and are being tested with two hotels. This is a significant scope expansion from the original pitch.

Turkey Reality Check

Despite regulatory deadlines, zero hotels signed in Turkey. Co-founders are asking hard questions about whether new markets will convert. "Systemic issues" being sorted out. This means CDE's discovery research must validate market readiness before recommending where to spend.

Positioning Breakthrough

Both Balaji and CB agreed: lead with efficiency/profitability, deliver sustainability as the outcome. "The Trojan horse" approach. Hotels don't care about sustainability for its own sake. They care about bottom line. Sustainability is the steak, efficiency is the sizzle. This will be a core brand discovery question.

New Tools to Showcase

Hotel BFF has built a renewable energy ROI calculator (enter hotel address, get solar potential + ROI estimate) and a biodiversity/natural capital calculator. These are strong demo-able features that could drive lead gen. Also developed architect/project manager training for pre-construction sustainability planning.

What CB Expects from CDE

  • Independent market research to validate which markets and products have real business potential
  • Research that goes beyond "everyone agrees sustainability is good" to actual conversion likelihood
  • Positioning guidance on how to talk about the product (sustainability vs. efficiency framing)
  • CB is doing his own competitive research on each product module and will share findings

Scope Boundaries

  • Paid advertising, social media, email marketing (Phase 2)
  • App redesign (app.hotelbff.com stays on Bubble.io)
Gray area to watch: CB asked whether CDE can tell them "which products have business potential and which country to go to." This crosses from marketing into business strategy consulting. CDE's discovery research will surface market data that informs these decisions, but the final product/market decisions belong to Hotel BFF. Frame it as: "We'll give you the data. You make the call."
